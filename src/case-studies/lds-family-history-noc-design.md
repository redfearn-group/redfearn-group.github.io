## Situation

LDS Family History's IT Operations Group needed a Network Operations Center, a working system for keeping eyes on their own infrastructure, and no existing design to build it from.

## What I Built

- **Consulted on the design of a web-based NOC** for LDS Family History's IT Operations Group, translating their operational monitoring needs into a system design the team could actually run.
- **Trained the team directly in NOC and UX/usability design and engineering principles and practices**, not just handing off a finished design but building the team's own capacity to extend and maintain it.

## The Hardest Problem

Two deliverables at once, in one short engagement: design something that worked, and teach the team the underlying practices well enough that they didn't need a consultant back in the room for the next change. Those goals pull against each other. A consultant optimizing purely for a polished handoff doesn't spend time teaching; one purely focused on teaching risks leaving without a working system. Both had to happen inside the same six months.

## Outcome

LDS Family History's IT Operations Group came out of the engagement with a working NOC design and the design and engineering practices to keep evolving it on their own, an early instance of a pattern that shows up throughout the later work: build the thing, but leave the team better equipped than you found them.

## Skills Demonstrated

NOC and systems monitoring design, UX and usability engineering, technical training and knowledge transfer, independent consulting.
