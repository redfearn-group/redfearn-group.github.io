## Situation

LDS Family History's IT Operations Group needed a Network Operations Center: a working system for keeping eyes on their own infrastructure. No existing design to build it from.

## What I Built

- **Consulted on the design of a web-based NOC** for LDS Family History's IT Operations Group, translating their operational monitoring needs into a system design the team could actually run.
- **Trained the team directly in NOC and UX/usability design and engineering principles and practices.** I didn't just hand off a finished design. I built their capacity to extend and maintain it themselves.

## The Hardest Problem

Two deliverables at once, in one short engagement: design something that worked, and teach the team the underlying practices well enough they didn't need a consultant back in the room for the next change. Those goals pull against each other. Optimize purely for a polished handoff and you skip the teaching. Optimize purely for teaching and you risk leaving without a working system. Both had to happen inside the same six months.

## Outcome

LDS Family History's IT Operations Group came out of the engagement with a working NOC design and the practices to keep evolving it on their own. It's an early instance of something I kept doing later: build the thing, and leave the team better equipped than you found them.

## Skills Demonstrated

NOC and systems monitoring design, UX and usability engineering, technical training and knowledge transfer, independent consulting.
